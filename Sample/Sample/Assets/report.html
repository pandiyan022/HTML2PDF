BACKGROUND
A Coriolis meter has no moving parts and there is nothing to wear or break. Therefore, in a clean fluid, the expectation is that the meter will not exhibit a calibration change over time.
Fluids that corrode, erode, or coat the flow tube can alter the mechanical characteristics of the meter and may change the meter’s calibration. Other mechanical damage (such as over pressurization) can also affect the meter’s calibration. Smart Meter Verification measures the mechanical characteristics of the meter and detects subtle changes that affect the calibration constants.

METHOD
Smart Meter Verification compares the mechanical properties of the meter at operating conditions to the factory measured values. Smart Meter Verification is independent of the process fluid in the meter. During meter commissioning, Micro Motion recommends performing Smart Meter Verification as part of the installation procedure. Micro Motion recommends running Smart Meter Verification periodically (at least once per month) using the scheduling feature in the transmitter.

ACTION
When the Smart Meter Verification test is completed, the result is reported as Pass, Fail, or Abort.
Pass
Smart Meter Verification does a statistical check between the factory baseline value and the current Smart Meter Verification result. Pass indicates that the two values are statistically the same.
Fail
The current SMV value is statistically different from the factory baseline value. If a fail result is obtained, SMV should be performed a second time. • If the meter passes a second test, the first result can be ignored. • If the meter fails the second test, the flow tubes may be damaged or coated. Use your process knowledge to determine the possibilities for damage and the appropriate actions for each. These actions might include cleaning the meter, or removing the meter from service and physically inspecting the tubes. At a minimum, you should perform a flow validation and density calibration.
Abort
A problem occurred with the meter verification test (e.g., process instability) or you stopped the test manually. See the flow meter user manual for a list of abort codes, a description of each code, and possible actions you can take in response.
